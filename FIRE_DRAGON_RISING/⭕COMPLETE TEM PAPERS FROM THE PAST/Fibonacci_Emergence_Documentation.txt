## 2. Fibonacci Emergence from Expansion Geometry

### 2.1 Pattern Stability and Growth
In EMF, expanding patterns must maintain stable overlap relationships. For two-dimensional growth, the areas of adjacent patterns must satisfy:
    A_{n+1} = A_n + A_{n-1}.
This relationship implies a corresponding rule for radii:
    R_{n+1}^2 = R_n^2 + R_{n-1}^2.

Similarly, in three dimensions, the volumes of adjacent spheres must adhere to:
    V_{n+1} = V_n + V_{n-1}.
This leads to the rule for radii:
    R_{n+1}^3 = R_n^3 + R_{n-1}^3.

### 2.2 Connection to the Golden Ratio
As the number of steps increases, the normalized radii approach a constant ratio. In 2D, this ratio converges to the golden ratio, φ ≈ 1.618. In 3D, the ratio approaches φ³ ≈ 1.465, the real solution to x³ = x² + 1.

### 2.3 Physical Implications
- **Quantization:** Expansion occurs in discrete, stable steps, mirroring quantum behaviors.
- **Scale Invariance:** Growth ratios are preserved under transformations, providing geometric stability.
- **Observability:** This geometric sequence suggests physical manifestations at atomic and cosmic scales.
